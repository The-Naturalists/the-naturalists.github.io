The Naturalists

IISc Undergraduate Biology Club

Welcome to the website of The Naturalists, the IISc undergraduate biology club. We are a small group of undergraduates, who are interested in biology and its different aspects and wish to explore the subject as much as we can. We aim to gain the most we can from the peer pool by arranging regular discussions among ourselves and presenting our views on different modern topics. Also, talks on different biological fields by professors will be arranged and we plan to go out occasionally on some trips.

Please mail to us, The Naturalists, about any idea or topic you find interesting and worth discussing in the club!

6th November, 2015

Talk by Kaivalya Molugu, 4th year undergraduate, titled, "Structural Understanding of Aryl Hydrocarbon Receptor (AHR), a potential drug target for cancer therapy". To be held in Room G20 of the Old Phyisics Building (OPB) at 5.30 pm.

Abstract

Aryl hydrocarbon receptor (AHR) is a cytosolic transcription factor that mediates cellular responses to diverse environmental chemicals and endogenous metabolites. It has been shown to play an important role in toxicity response, and normal immune and cardiovascular functions, with important implication for cancer and autoimmune diseases. Increasing experimental data provides substantial support for an association between abnormal AHR function and cancer, implicating that AHR might be a potential drug-interfering target. Determining the structure of AHR may provide usefu insights into this potential. However, the structure of AHR has not yet been determined completely due to the various problems associated with its stability and solubility during the process of crystallization to determine its structure. In this talk, I will discuss about attempts made to tackle this problem.
